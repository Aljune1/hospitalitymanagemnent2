Sustainability and Conservation Efforts

1. Typhoon Risk Management Framework

Early Warning Systems: Establish partnerships with local weather agencies to receive real-time updates and alerts about approaching typhoons. The park can set up a notification system for visitors and staff to take preventive action well in advance.

Infrastructure Reinforcement: Ensure all buildings, camping areas, and other structures are typhoon-resistant by reinforcing roofs, windows, and doors. Trees and plants should be checked for stability to prevent accidents during strong winds.

Evacuation Plans: Create clear, accessible evacuation routes for guests and staff, with emergency shelters located in safer areas. Regular drills should be conducted to ensure everyone knows the procedures during an emergency.

Emergency Supplies: Stock essential items such as first aid kits, non-perishable food, drinking water, flashlights, and power banks. Ensure that staff members are trained in emergency response procedures.

2. Sustainability Initiatives

Eco-friendly Infrastructure: Utilize sustainable building materials, energy-efficient lighting, and rainwater collection systems to minimize the park's environmental footprint.

Waste Management: Implement waste segregation and recycling programs throughout the park. Encourage guests to reduce, reuse, and recycle their waste, especially plastics, to prevent environmental harm.

Conservation Education: Organize workshops and informational sessions about local ecosystems, such as the endangered species of the Malumpati Watershed and the importance of preserving the region's biodiversity.

Restoration Projects: Initiate reforestation or mangrove restoration projects around the park to mitigate the impacts of natural disasters like typhoons and reduce the risk of soil erosion.
